About Me


Hi! I'm Zachary Heathcock, a Computer Engineering Graduate from the University of Memphis with a strong academic background (GPA 3.72) and a passion for hardware solutions to real world problems. My experience spans both hardware and software, from designing relay kits and bi-directional energy controllers to developing neural networks for real-world object recognition.
Currently, I’m an engineering intern at Peak+, where I contribute to system design, site performance indexing, and productivity optimization through automation with Microsoft VBA and Excel. I also collaborate with cross-functional teams to assist in engineering custom cooling solutions based on client constraints.
My technical skill set includes Python, VHDL, MATLAB, VBA, and JavaScript, with hands-on experience using tools like TensorFlow, Keras, PyTorch, LTSpice, and PSpice. I'm also an active member of Tau Beta Pi and IEEE-HKN, and a proud recipient of the University of Memphis Cecil C. Humphreys Presidential Scholarship.
Regardless of the application or job sector, I enjoy solving multifaceted real-world problems at the intersection of hardware and software.
