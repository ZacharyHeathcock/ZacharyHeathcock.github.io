Battery Energy Storage System
A battery energy system built with a Raspberry Pi and bidirectional buck-boost converter, designed for energy flow control with real-time data.
This project was designed for fulfillment of my senior design class for the University of Memphis engineering program. It was developed with 3 other Electrical Engineering students and one dual Electrical and Computer student. This project utilized a Raspberry PI running a custom python script that utilized Particle Swarm Optimization. The PI receives information from the source, load, and battery, and used this information to change the duty cycle of the buck-boost. These changes would change the relationship of Vin and Vout therefore increasing or decreasing the energy flowing into the battery or out of the battery.

Autonomous Robot Design for Nuclear Waste​ Handling and Storage​
This project was a prototype design project for the Mechatronics class at the University of Memphis.
The proposed solution includes radiation shielding space for all critical electronics. It also allows for standard decontamination spraying, due to the computational compartment, which is air tight to prevent radioactive particle infiltration. The sensory equipment is radiation resistant, and all critical equipment has space to include additional radiation shielding if required. The system is able to operate without human intervention, due to the binocular camera array. Low level radiation would allow for the LiDAR sensor to be used, increasing terrain following capabilities. This could be used before the waste is collected to train the system on the routes it will use. The system is capable of handling waste barrels and spherical objects, and also storing them due to the manipulation arm and storage cart.
